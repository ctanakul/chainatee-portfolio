MECHANICAL POWER GLOVE

 

 

Brachial Plexus Injury (BPI) is a common motorbike injury. This injury is caused by damage to the brachial plexus network, leading to a weakness or loss of movement in a shoulder, arm or hand.

As a research assistant in Human Robotics Lab, Chulalongkorn University, my team and I aim to initiate the mechanical power glove with passive movement to grasp objects.

My role in the team was to initiate a static wrist holder to hold the wrist while grasping. Since the glove is actuated by tendons, the wrist holder must allow all tendons to pass through without any sharp edges that could cut them. To retain mobility, the device must be low in weight. Moreover, it needs to be adjustable with different wrist sizes.

Challenges

  • must allow tendons to pass through without being cut by sharp edges
  • must be low in weight
  • adjustable with different wrist sizes

Solutions

  • bend holes to form with the angle of fingers
  • design the part to be as thin as possible
  • use a guitar knob to open and close the assembly which is also user-friendly
  • design with curve to support a wrist well
