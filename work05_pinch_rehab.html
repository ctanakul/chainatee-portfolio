PINCHING REHAB MECHANISM

 

 

Many patients who suffer from stroke are unable to perform some important hand movements. One of them is the tip pinch. This research aims to design and develop a simple mechanism for pinch movement rehabilitation. The device has one degree of freedom that couples the motion between the index and thumb joints. The actuated joint is driven by a dc motor with a round cable system. The mechanism aims to adapt with regular hand sizes based on the average bone size. The coupling transmission ratio between 2 joints is calculated to ensure a proper motion for pulp contact. The prototype was developed and tested by non-patient subjects. The average force for pinch action is about 1.6 N.

My responsibilities

  • worked with CAD and ordered materials
