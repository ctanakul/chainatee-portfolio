PINCHING REHAB MECHANISM

 

 

Many stroke sufferers are unable to perform some hand movements important in daily lives. One of them is tip pinch. This research aims to design and develop a simple mechanism for pinch movement rehabilitation. The device has one degree of freedom that couples the motion between index and thumb joints. The actuated joint is driven by a dc motor with round cable system. The mechanism aims to adapt with regular hand sizes based on the average bone's size according to literatures. The coupling transmission ratio between 2 joints is calculated to ensure a proper motion for pulp contact. The prototype was developed and tested by non-patient subjects. The average force for pinch action is about 1.6 N.

My responsibilities

  • reviewed literatures
  • designed, optimized and simulated system in CATIA
  • worked with CAD and ordered materials
  • wrote the English version of this paper to be presented at TSME-ICOME(Thai Society of Mechanical Engineering) 2015

This project paper was published in the 6th TSME International Conference on Mechanical Engineering 16-18 December 2015.
